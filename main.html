    INDIA ON THE EVE OF MUGHAL CONQUEST

    India in the 16th century presented a picture of political disunity and disarray.

    The Delhi Sultanate had shrunk in size and power, with several regions breaking free from its control.

    The governor of Punjab, Daulat Khan Lodi, had declared his independence from the Delhi Sultanate; so had the governors of Bengal, Sindh, Multan and Jaunpur.

    The rulers of Mewar, Malwa, Gujarat and Khandesh no longer owed allegiance to the Delhi Sultanate.

    In the Deccan and the South, powerful regional kingdoms like the Bahamani kingdom and mighty Vijayanagara Empire ruled.

    The Mughals were the powerful descendants of the Mongols. (Mughal is the Persian word for 'Mongol'.)

    Under the inspired leadership of Babur, they took advantage og the absence of a strong central power to invade India and established Mughal rule in Delhi.

    The Mughal Empire in India lasted from the 16th century to mid 19th century CE, i.e., for around 250 years.

    In the course of these 250 years, the Mughals expanded their empire to cover almost the whole of the Indian subcontinent and left a permanent mark on the society, culture, art and architecture of India.
